🎯 Problem Statement (The Core Truth)
The merchant loses revenue at exactly two moments they can't see clearly:

A user at checkout gets overwhelmed by 6+ payment choices, misses applicable offers, and either makes a suboptimal payment or abandons
A user who already left gets a generic "you left something in your cart" email that converts at under 5%

These aren't UX problems. They're intelligence gaps — the checkout has no reasoning layer, and the recovery has no diagnosis layer.

📋 Form Answers

❓What problem are you trying to solve?

Indian digital checkout has two critical revenue leakage points that remain unsolved. First, at the moment of payment, consumers face fragmented complexity — multiple cards, overlapping bank offers, EMI options, and wallet balances — with no system to reason over them. They either choose suboptimally (leaving cashback and offers unclaimed) or abandon due to decision fatigue. Second, when users drop off mid-checkout, merchants have no way to diagnose why the abandonment happened — price sensitivity, payment method friction, offer confusion, or trust — and therefore send generic recovery nudges that fail. Combined, these two gaps cost merchants an estimated 60–70% of potential checkout revenue on every session that doesn't convert cleanly.


❓Proposed solution?

We are building CheckoutIQ — a merchant-side agentic intelligence layer that operates across the full checkout lifecycle via two coordinated agent systems.
Layer 1 — Smart Checkout Agent (at checkout): When a user reaches payment, a multi-agent pipeline activates silently. Specialized agents independently evaluate the user's available cards, active bank and merchant offers, EMI cost-benefit, and wallet balances. A conflict resolution agent handles mutually exclusive offer stacking. A final decision agent synthesizes recommendations into one optimal payment configuration, surfaced to the user as a single "Smart Apply" action — with a transparent reasoning trail showing exactly what was applied and why.
Layer 2 — Abandonment Recovery Agent (post-dropout): When a user exits without completing payment, a dropout analysis agent diagnoses the likely cause from behavioral signals (time spent on payment screen, methods hovered, cart value vs. offer gap). A recovery crafter agent then generates a personalized re-engagement — a targeted offer, an EMI breakdown if cart value was the barrier, or a simplified payment link — delivered as a nudge that routes directly back into the Pine Labs checkout session.
Together, these form a complete checkout intelligence system: maximize conversion at the moment, recover it when it fails.


❓How will AI or automation be used?

AI is the core reasoning layer, not a wrapper. The system uses a CrewAI-orchestrated multi-agent pipeline where each agent has a scoped role and uses an LLM to reason — not just retrieve:

Card Selection Agent — reasons over reward structures, cashback rates, and card-specific offer eligibility against the cart contents
Offer Eligibility Agent — parses active merchant and bank offer conditions, checks user eligibility, expiry, and minimum order constraints
EMI Analysis Agent — calculates effective cost of EMI vs. upfront payment factoring in interest, cashback, and user's likely cash flow context
Wallet Optimization Agent — determines optimal partial payment splits across wallets and cards
Conflict Resolution Agent — handles cases where offers are mutually exclusive, reasons over trade-offs
Decision Agent — synthesizes all agent outputs into a single ranked recommendation with explanation

For abandonment recovery, a Dropout Diagnosis Agent interprets behavioral signals to infer abandonment cause, and a Recovery Crafter Agent generates a contextually appropriate re-engagement strategy — not a template, but a reasoned response to that specific user's specific friction point.
No hardcoded rules. Each agent reasons from context, making the system adaptive to new offers, new payment methods, and new user behaviors without re-engineering.


❓How will your solution interact with Pine Labs payments?

CheckoutIQ integrates with Pine Labs at three points:

Offer & Payment Method Catalog — The agent pipeline fetches active merchant offers, available payment instruments, and EMI configurations from Pine Labs' checkout APIs at session initiation, giving agents live data to reason over
Checkout Session Execution — Once the Decision Agent produces its optimal configuration, it is applied directly to the Pine Labs checkout session — pre-selecting the payment method, activating the identified offer, and configuring EMI terms — before the user confirms
Session Drop Signal + Recovery Re-entry — Pine Labs checkout session lifecycle events (incomplete sessions, timeouts) trigger the Abandonment Recovery pipeline. Recovered users are routed back into a pre-configured Pine Labs checkout session with the friction point already resolved



This means Pine Labs is not just the payment rail — it is the live data source, the execution surface, and the re-entry point for the entire intelligence layer.

❓Business impact & what makes your idea unique?

Business Impact:

Cart abandonment costs Indian e-commerce an estimated $10B+ annually. Even a 15% improvement in recovery rate is transformative for a merchant's bottom line
Offer under-utilization means consumers leave money on the table every transaction — merchants who solve this see measurably higher customer satisfaction and repeat purchase rates
CheckoutIQ gives Pine Labs a differentiated value proposition: not just a payment processor, but the smartest checkout layer in the ecosystem

What makes it unique:

No competitor has a reasoning layer at checkout. Razorpay, Cashfree, PayU all show offers passively. None reason over them on the user's behalf
Abandonment recovery that diagnoses before it responds — industry standard is time-delayed generic emails. CheckoutIQ infers why someone left and responds to that specific reason
Multi-agent architecture means it scales with complexity — as offer catalogs grow and payment methods multiply, the agent system handles it without rule re-engineering
Explainability as trust — users see exactly why a payment combination was chosen, building confidence rather than black-box anxiety
